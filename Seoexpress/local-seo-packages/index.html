What's Included

What Every Local SEO Package Includes

The workload changes between packages. The fundamentals do not. Every Local SEO Express campaign is built around the same goal: help more nearby customers find, trust and contact your business.

Local SEO Audit

We start by reviewing your complete local search presence—website, Google Business Profile, rankings, competitors, service pages, location pages, citations, reviews, backlinks, technical SEO and tracking.

This gives us a clear starting point. Without an audit, an agency is guessing. And you should not pay someone to guess.

Competitor Research

We do not only look at what SEO experts say should work. We look at the businesses already ranking in your market.

We compare their websites, service pages, location coverage, reviews, citations, backlinks, content, authority and Google Business Profiles—then identify what they have that you do not.

Local Keyword Research

The biggest keyword is not always the best place to start. Sometimes a lower-volume keyword can rank faster and generate more qualified leads.

We look for the best combination of search volume, commercial intent, competition, local relevance, service value, ranking difficulty and revenue potential.

Google Business Profile Optimization

Your Google Business Profile can generate calls, website visits and direction requests before someone ever visits your website.

We optimize primary and secondary categories, services, products, business description, hours, service areas, photos, attributes, website links, appointment links, Q&A and tracking links.

Google Maps SEO

Google Maps rankings are influenced by more than profile completion. Your website, reviews, citations, location relevance and authority can all affect how visible you become.

Our Google Maps work includes local relevance improvements, service-area optimization, website support, citation consistency, review growth, local authority, Maps rank tracking and competitor comparisons.

Website SEO

Your website tells Google what you do, where you do it and which searches your pages should appear for.

We improve title tags, meta descriptions, headings, page copy, internal links, service relevance, location relevance, structured data, image optimization, mobile usability, page speed and conversion elements.

Service-Page Optimization

One generic services page is rarely enough. If a service has meaningful search demand and commercial value, it may deserve its own page.

For example, a plumbing company may need separate pages for drain cleaning, water-heater repair, sewer-line repair, emergency plumbing and leak detection.

Location and Service-Area Pages

Serving a city does not automatically make your website relevant to that city. You need content that clearly demonstrates what you offer there, which areas you serve, why your business is relevant and why local customers should choose you.

We do not create dozens of weak pages by changing one city name. We build useful pages designed to deserve visibility.

Citation Building and Cleanup

Your business information appears across directories, maps, review sites and data providers. When that information is incorrect or inconsistent, it can create confusion.

We audit and improve important citations involving business name, address, phone number, website, hours, categories and business description. See our citation building services for standalone projects.

Review Strategy

Reviews can influence rankings and determine whether a customer clicks your business or the company underneath it.

We help you build a sustainable review process around when to ask, how to ask, where to send customers, how to follow up, how to respond and how to track growth—without fake reviews or policy violations.

Local Content

Content should have a reason to exist. We create and improve content that supports core services, target locations, customer questions, buying decisions, internal linking, topical authority and local relevance.

That may include service pages, location pages, cost guides, comparison pages, FAQs, local resources and problem-and-solution content.

Local Link Building

Links remain one of the signals that can separate an established business from a weaker competitor. But link quality matters.

We look for opportunities connected to local organizations, trade associations, suppliers, business partners, sponsorships, community websites, local media and industry publications.

Technical SEO

Even good content can struggle when technical problems prevent Google from properly crawling or understanding the website.

We inspect indexing, broken links, redirects, duplicate content, canonical tags, site structure, mobile performance, page speed, structured data, internal linking and crawlability.

Tracking and Reporting

Rankings matter—but they are not the final goal. We want to know whether improved visibility is producing business.

Depending on your setup, we can track Google Maps visibility, organic rankings, phone calls, form submissions, website traffic, direction requests, booked appointments, lead sources and conversion rates.

Package Differences

What Changes Between Packages?

The higher packages do not simply contain more random tasks. They provide more of the resources required to compete.

Number of Locations

A one-location company is simpler to optimize than a business with offices across several markets. Each location may need its own Google Business Profile, unique location content, local citations, review management, rank tracking and market research.

Number of Services

A business with three services may need only a small group of optimized pages. A company offering 25 services may need more keyword research, more pages, more content, more internal linking and more tracking.

Market Competition

Ranking in a smaller town may require better optimization and consistent reviews. Ranking in a major city may require extensive content, stronger links, more reviews and a longer campaign.

Current Website Condition

Some businesses already have a strong website. Others have thin pages, duplicate content, poor structure or weak conversion elements. A campaign that starts with a broken foundation requires more work.

Authority Gap

Sometimes the page is properly optimized, but competitors still have significantly more authority—more referring domains, reviews, citations and historical trust. In that situation, the campaign needs authority building.

By Business Type

Single-Location & Multi-Location Packages

Your location structure determines how the campaign is scoped, priced and executed.

Single Location

Single-Location Local SEO Packages

A single-location package is designed around one primary business location or service area. It may focus on one Google Business Profile, core local keywords, primary services, nearby service areas, local citations, reviews, website optimization and local links.

This is usually the simplest local SEO structure. But difficulty still depends on the market. One location in a small town may require less work than one location in New York, Miami or Los Angeles.

Multi Location

Multi-Location Local SEO Packages

Multi-location SEO requires a scalable strategy. Each location must be treated as a real local business presence—with separate profiles, location pages, ranking markets, citations, review profiles and reporting.

The biggest mistake is creating dozens of nearly identical pages and expecting them all to rank. Each location needs enough unique relevance and authority to compete in its own market.

Buyer Beware

Why Cheap Local SEO Packages Often Fail

There is nothing wrong with wanting affordable SEO. There is a problem when the price is so low that the work cannot realistically be completed.

A $99 monthly package may promise hundreds of backlinks, weekly blog posts, first-page rankings and full website optimization. That sounds impressive—until you ask how one company can perform all of that for less than the cost of a single skilled hour.

1

The Work Is Automated

Your business gets submitted to low-quality sites, spun content is published and automated reports are sent each month.

2

The Important Work Is Missing

The package includes easy tasks but leaves out the work that actually takes time: research, content, strategy, link acquisition, conversion tracking and technical fixes.

3

Nothing Meaningful Happens

The agency completes a checklist. Your rankings do not improve. Your calls do not increase. And six months later, you still do not know what you paid for.

Affordable should mean efficient and correctly scoped. It should not mean imaginary deliverables at an impossible price.

Our Packages Do Not Guarantee Rankings

No legitimate SEO company controls Google. That means no honest agency can guarantee that your business will rank first.

What we can do is identify why competitors are outranking you, improve the signals holding you back, target realistic opportunities, measure progress and build a stronger long-term search presence.

Be cautious of any package that guarantees a specific position by a specific date—especially before the agency has inspected your market.

How Long Before You See Results?

There is no single timeline for every business. Some lower-competition keywords may begin moving relatively quickly. Competitive markets can take longer because your business must close larger gaps in content, reviews, links and authority.

  • How strong your competitors are
  • How much work is completed each month
  • How quickly changes are indexed
  • The difficulty of your target keywords

We will give you a clearer expectation after reviewing your market.

Making the Right Choice

How to Choose the Right Local SEO Package

Do not choose based only on the number of items in the table. Choose based on what your business needs to compete.

How competitive is my market?

The stronger the competition, the more content and authority you may need.

How many services do I want to target?

More services usually require more pages and broader keyword coverage.

How many locations do I serve?

Multiple locations require separate research, content and tracking.

What is the condition of my website?

A strong website may need optimization. A weak website may need rebuilding or substantial new content.

How much authority do my competitors have?

If competitors have stronger backlink profiles and established brands, your campaign may need ongoing authority building.

What can one new customer be worth?

A campaign that generates one additional high-value client may pay for itself very differently from a campaign for a lower-value service.

The cheapest package is not automatically the best value. The right package is the one that can realistically produce a return.
